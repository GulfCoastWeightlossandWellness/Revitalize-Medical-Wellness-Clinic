The hesitation most first-time aesthetic patients feel before booking is not about the treatments themselves — it is about the experience they do not know how to anticipate. What does the consultation look like? Will they feel pressured? What does the treatment actually feel like? What if they do not like the result?

These are reasonable concerns. The purpose of this guide is to answer all of them directly, based on how the aesthetic consultation and treatment experience at Revitalize actually works.

## Before you arrive — what to do

**For injectable treatments (Botox, Dysport, fillers):**

Avoid blood thinners — aspirin, ibuprofen, fish oil, and vitamin E supplements — for 48 to 72 hours before your appointment to reduce bruising risk. Acetaminophen is fine if you need pain relief.

Avoid alcohol for 24 hours before. Alcohol is a mild vasodilator and anticoagulant that increases bruising.

Arrive with a clean face — no heavy makeup. You do not need to remove makeup before leaving home, but the treatment area will be cleansed before any injection.

**For skin treatments (microneedling, laser, peels):**

Follow any specific pre-treatment instructions your provider gave at consultation. These vary by treatment and skin condition — they were given to you at the consultation for a reason.

Arrive without active tanning. Recent sun exposure affects skin sensitivity and treatment outcomes.

**For any appointment:**

Bring a list of your current medications and supplements, including anything topical you are using on your skin. Some ingredients (retinoids, exfoliants) affect treatment safety and timing.

Know what your main concerns are. You do not need to arrive with a detailed treatment plan — that is the provider's job — but knowing what bothers you most helps direct a productive consultation.

## The consultation — what actually happens

The consultation at Revitalize is a clinical assessment, not a sales pitch. The provider will:

Ask about your concerns — what you are bothered by, what you have tried before, and what outcome you are hoping to achieve.

Assess your facial anatomy or skin condition — muscle activity patterns, volume distribution, skin quality, movement patterns — before making any recommendation.

Explain what is achievable and what is not for your specific anatomy. You will hear honest answers about realistic expectations, timelines, and limitations.

Present a recommendation with the reasoning behind it. If you are not ready to proceed or want to think about it, that is fine — there is no obligation to commit at the consultation.

Answer your questions. No concern is too minor to raise.

What the consultation is not: a consultation where you are told what you need before being examined, quoted a flat package before your anatomy is assessed, or made to feel that proceeding immediately is expected.

## The treatment experience

For [injectable treatments](/services/neuromodulators):

The treatment area is cleansed. For lip or filler procedures, topical anesthetic is applied and allowed to sit for 20 to 30 minutes. For Botox and Dysport in most areas, topical numbing is available but often not necessary.

The injections take 10 to 20 minutes for most treatments. The sensation is brief, localized pressure or stinging — not sustained pain.

You leave looking largely normal for neuromodulators (some minor redness at injection points) or mildly swollen for fillers.

For skin treatments:

The treatment area is cleansed and prepared per the specific protocol.

The procedure time varies: 30 minutes for microneedling, 30 to 60 minutes for CO2 laser, 15 to 20 minutes for a chemical peel application.

Your provider will explain what to expect immediately after and give you written post-care instructions before you leave.

## After your appointment — the first 24 to 48 hours

**Botox and Dysport:** Minimal care required. Avoid lying flat for four hours, avoid strenuous exercise for the day, avoid rubbing the treated area. Return to normal activity the next day.

**Fillers:** Mild swelling and possible bruising. Avoid strenuous exercise, extreme heat, and alcohol for 24 to 48 hours. Use arnica if bruising occurs. A two-week follow-up is offered to assess the result.

**Microneedling:** 24 to 72 hours of redness and mild sensitivity. Apply the post-care products as directed. Avoid sun exposure.

**CO2 laser:** Five to ten days of significant healing. Follow the post-care protocol exactly. Plan for reduced social and professional activity during the healing phase.

## What to do if you do not like the result

First — wait. Neuromodulator results develop over two weeks; filler settles over one to two weeks; skin treatment results continue to develop for weeks to months. Assessing the result before two weeks is premature.

Second — book your two-week follow-up if you booked a filler or first-time neuromodulator treatment. This appointment is specifically for reviewing the result and addressing any areas that need adjustment.

Third — contact your provider directly if something looks or feels significantly wrong. A responsible aesthetic practice wants to know when a result is not meeting expectations.
